Optimisation works by including a cost for the 2AB terms of the x(i-1),x(I),x(i+1) coordinates and the track widths.
Cost increases when angle is high because the reward decreases (small subtraction), the a term decides the position of the car along the line of the segment. 
0 is innermost wall and 1 is outermost. 
Matrix is built with number of segments, diagonally down
(X X 0 0 0)
(X X X 0 0)
(0 X X X 0)
X being the angle calculation
Acceleration = x(i+1) - 2*x(I) + x(i-1)
x(I) = Inner + a(position of car along segment)*Width(at segment)
For matrix, the middle will be (-2*x(I))^2
Minimise (Accel)^2. So when expanding (A+B+C)^2 where A,B,C are the x neighbour terms, there are two types of terms.
Squared term: B*B, angle of vector itself (0 degrees) as cos(0) = 1
Cross term (neighbours): 2*B*C.
Middle term * a(2) = Front term * a(1) + End term * a(3)
Middle term is the penalty from moving away from zero.
Neighbour is the reward for moving with them. 
Solver wants to get the cost as close to 0 as possible.
H-matrix * a-matrix (location of vehicle along line) + B-matrix = 0
B-matrix represents the curvature of the inner wall of the track
B = 2 * xin(i+1) - 2*xin(I) + xin(i-1) (Curvature of innerwall) * Outerpoint(I) - Innerpoint(I) (Direction of track width)
a is worked out by doing H^-1 * -B
